Sectors / Mining & Resources

⛏️ Mining & Natural Resources

Guinea is a global mining giant — home to the world's largest bauxite reserves, exceptional gold, iron ore (Simandou), and diamond deposits.

GUINÉE RESOURCES

Guinea's Extraordinary Mineral Wealth

The Republic of Guinea is endowed with one of the richest subsoils in the world, making it a strategic destination for international mining investors.

⛏️

Bauxite

Guinea holds two-thirds of the world's known bauxite reserves — over 40 billion tonnes. The Boké, Kindia, and Fria regions are home to world-class deposits. Companies such as Compagnie des Bauxites de Guinée (CBG), Guinea Alumina Corporation (GAC), and Société Minière de Boké (SMB) operate large-scale extraction, making Guinea the world's leading bauxite exporter.

Au

Gold

Guinea is part of the West African Gold Belt, one of the richest auriferous corridors in the world. The Siguiri Basin (Haute-Guinée) and Lero-Nzérékoré regions host significant gold deposits. AngloGold Ashanti and other international majors have long recognized Guinea's exceptional gold potential. Industrial and artisanal gold mining coexist across vast exploration zones.

Fe

Iron Ore

The Simandou massif contains what is considered the world's largest untapped high-grade iron ore deposit — estimated at over 2 billion tonnes of ore with 65%+ Fe content. This landmark project, under development by international consortia, is expected to transform Guinea's economy and become a top global iron ore source.

💎

Diamonds

Guinea's Macenta and Kissidougou regions in Guinée Forestière contain alluvial and kimberlitic diamond deposits. Though production is currently dominated by artisanal mining, significant industrial exploration potential remains untapped. BCRG-approved diamond dealers and export frameworks are in place to support investment.

🪨

Other Minerals

Guinea's subsoil also contains chromite, nickel, cobalt, copper, graphite, and rutile (titanium). The country's geological diversity makes it one of Africa's most promising mineral frontiers — with vast areas still under-explored and open to strategic partnerships with international mining companies.

Sectors / Agriculture & Agro-Industry

🌾 Agriculture & Agro-Industry

Guinea is blessed with exceptional agricultural potential — 6.2 million hectares of arable land, abundant rainfall, and ideal tropical conditions for a wide range of food and industrial crops.

Guinea's Agricultural Potential

Despite exceptional natural endowments, only 20% of Guinea's arable land is cultivated — representing a transformative investment opportunity in large-scale agriculture and agro-processing.

🌺
Cash Crops — Export
Guinea is a significant producer of coffee, cacao, cashew nuts, and palm oil. Guinée Forestière is the main agricultural region, with exceptional conditions for tropical export crops. PRINCAM facilitates large-scale plantation and agro-processing investment.
🌽
Food Crops
Rice, maize, cassava, yam, and vegetables are the main food crops. Guinea imports significant quantities of staple foods despite its agricultural potential — representing a major opportunity for food production investment and import substitution.
🐟
Fisheries & Aquaculture
Guinea's 300km Atlantic coastline and major rivers offer exceptional fisheries potential. Industrial fishing, artisanal fishing modernization, and freshwater aquaculture are priority investment areas supported by the Guinean government.
🏭
Agro-Processing
Value-added agro-industrial transformation — rice mills, palm oil processing, cashew shelling, fruit transformation — creates high-value opportunities at the intersection of agriculture and industry. PRINCAM structures agro-processing JV partnerships.

Land & Investment Framework

Guinea operates under a documented agricultural land access framework. PRINCAM GROUP facilitates land negotiation with local communities and government authorities, ensuring compliant and sustainable investment structures aligned with OHADA law and international ESG standards.
